Who We Are

Applied Media Promotions (AMP) is a student-staffed strategic communications firm operated by the Fine and Applied arts Department at Parkland College. We provide student with the oppportunity to enhace their writing, critical thinking, creative, and technical skills by producing promotional materials for nonprofit organizations in the Champaign-Urbana community.

Our History

AMP launched in Spring 2014 with two communication students, one graphic design student, and one client. We met in various classrooms around campus to develop a branding strategy for what was then an entrepreneurial lecture series. It has since become the Parkland Entrepreneurial Network, a support system for budding entrepreneurs.

Following the success of that “pilot” semester, we officially launched in Fall 2014 and moved into a renovated storage space in the lower level of the D wing. Thanks to the recruitment efforts of our three existing members, the number of students who began working with us quadrupled. We were able to complete multiple projects for clients on campus, including the Presentation Center (formerly the Speech Lab), PCTV, the Study Abroad Program, Engineering Science Technologies, the Business Club, and the Staerkel Planetarium. We took on our first nonprofit client, Wesley Food Pantries, in Spring 2015 and now work exclusively with nonprofit clients.

AMP has continued to grow steadily over the past several semesters and also continues to expand the number of services we are able to provide to clients. In Fall 2016, we began offering web design and development through a partnership with students from Computer Science and Information Technology.  In Spring 2016, we moved into X109, a larger, more professional space and look forward connecting even more students with more clients.
